All About Reality Podcast

Hosted by Matt Goodwin and Luke Patrick O’Connell, the All About Reality Podcast is the only podcast with exclusive information for General Managers who play their fantasy football leagues on Reality Sports Online (“RSO”). Two veteran RSO GMs themselves, Matt and Luke are now in Season 3 of the podcast, having hosted an impressive number of famous folks from the fantasy community. A little more about our two hosts:

Matt Goodwin (aka Goody due to the plethora of Matt’s in the fantasy football community) is in his 8  season as a Reality Sports Online GM and third season as creator/co-host for All About Reality podcast for Reality Sports Online. Goody also contributed for numberFire for several years on the NFL and College Basketball writer, including writing game previews, player analysis, and deep-dive statistical analysis such as “Should I bench my fantasy starters on Thursday nights?” He is an avid sports fan from Cleveland, Ohio who would count a Cleveland Indians World Series victory a close second behind getting married to his wife Renee and the births of his children, Jory (10 year old son) and Lainie (5 year old daughter). Goody loves mid 90’s hip-hop (A Tribe Called Quest, The Roots, De La Soul, and Nas still occupy top of rotation spots), playing pick-up hoops (he claims to still have three point range, but focuses on his mid-range game, cerebral passing, and rebounding exceedingly well for 5’9 Jewish guy while taking approximately 10 dribbles a calendar year), and traveling. Favorite players across sports include Francisco Lindor, Lebron James, Cooper Kupp and any Vikings WR or Chiefs RB (based on previous fantasy success). Favorite movies include Glengarry Glen Ross, Trading Places, Shawshank Redemption and anything with non-golf sports in them. Favorite TV shows include I’m Sorry, Silicon Valley, Veep, and Shark Tank. You’ll often find Goody referencing quotes or naming podcast episodes from pop culture, some more obscure than others. Goody works as a Corporate Finance Manager in Bellevue, WA. Along with co-host and now close friend Luke Patrick, one of his biggest fantasy accomplishments was going undefeated as a 15-0 champion in a 16 team RSO Superflex in the podcast league’s first year as well as all the listeners/Twitter followers who have benefitted from our advice.

Luke Patrick fell in love the Reality Sports Online after the requisite trash talk and provocations from some friends and frenemies in the “home league” and after two decades of playing fantasy football finds it to be the most splendid experience.   A sports fan of places as varied as his upbringing, he cheers for the Vikings, Jazz, Nationals, and as a proud #girldad is usually only fines victory in the sweet embrace of the USWNT.  Talking fantasy football with Goody is a source of great joy and levity after days teaching philosophy and theology to some of the nation’s best and brightest. After a one-week reign on top of #SFB8, Luke has taken to analysis and game theory on Reality Sports Online and Settlers of Catan.  Musings on sports and obscure Irish comedy can be found on twitter @fantasyDocOC.

Season 3

Episode 23: On the Contrary: Talking contrarian strategies with RotoViz’ Shawn Siegele

With auctions starting to happen and training camp underway, Luke and Goody welcome Shawn Siegele, famed Zero RB enthusiast and industry-wide contrarian to talk about his recent articles and strategies. Some topics include how to do Zero RB in Reality Sports Online, how to identify players at a discount who may perform equal/better than their higher priced peers, and what TJ Hockenson’s value is in RSO Leagues.

Episode 22: The Plandemic with Matt, Kyle, and Stephen – How to navigate Coronavirus in RSO

RSO Founders Matt Papson and Stephen Wendell and Chief Technology Officer join Goody and Luke to take all questions regarding platform related options regarding COVID-19. How many games constitutes an RSO season per the platform? How does the contract tolling work for players like Damien Williams who have opted out? This and many other questions/scenarios are addressed for league commissioners and GMs to navigate how to best manage their teams/leagues in this unprecedented season.

Also, we introduce a new sponsor, Fantasy Points. Check out the podcast for a special offer for a discount on a Fantasy Points subscription.

Episode 21: Entering the Funhouse with Sal Leto

Goody and Luke are back with Sal Leto, anchor of the SFBPodathon and the FF Funhouse Podcast. Sal announces some big news and the three talk about some offseason happenings, including players opting out due to COVID-19 concerns and his future involvement in the Friends of Reality Podcast Guest league.

Episode 20: Canton Bound with Travis May from 

The boys are back in town and recording another power-packed episode with Travis May, host of the newly-minted College to Canton podcast and long-time Rotoviz analyst and host. The three talk about the vaunted 2021 class and a Top 5 as of now, focus on devy strategy a bit and then pivot to dynasty strategy and Reality Sports Online topics, including potential turnaround players.

Episode 19: Great Scott! Reeling in the big (Scott) Fish 

Luke and Goody, in the midst of being entrenched in their Scott Fish Bowl X drafts talk to the man himself, the great Scott Fish about Fish Bowl, Reality Sports Online, what makes the best Fish Bowl candidate, and Goody shares his experience in being in a league with ESPN’s Matthew Berry.

Before that, Goody starts with an important message to our audience.

Episode 18: All In and All About Cerality with the Athletic’s Jake Ciely  

Jake Ciely of The Athletic joins Luke and Goody and breaks down a lot of players in terms of finding sleepers and value, most over-drafted players and his dynasty rankings, all in a Reality Sports Online lens. Then, the three talk a little bit of Scott Fish Bowl, how Jake got his start, and then Jake talks about one of his real passions: ranking cereal.

For those who haven’t subscribed to The Athletic yet, go to one of Jake’s articles for the 4th of July sale for 50% off and subscribe. This is the best deal around all year for The Athletic.

Episode 17: Announcing the pick – 2020 Rookie draft analysis with a bevy of RSO analsyts

Goody and Luke are back talking 2020 rookie drafts and analyzing what’s happened in the 2020 Writer’s League Rookie Draft and the Podcast League as well. RSO stalwarts Bob Cowper and Bernard Faller, who had several first rounders in the Writer’s League Rookie Draft, hop on to analyze and share strategy.

A special announcement takes place via #AnnounceThePick as we introduce Ryan Krauchick, an RSO GM behind the campaign, who hopped on the panel to discuss how this charitable movement came about and what organizations are involved as well as his rookie strategy.

Episode 16: Six Underground with RotoWorld’s Matt Kelley

In an episode named as an ode to an amazing Sneaker Pimps song, Matt Kelley, aka “The Podfather” from RotoUnderworld joins Goody and Luke to answer some of the toughest Reality Sports Online decision points. Should you pick up a $20MM 5th year rookie extension on Leonard Fournette, Dalvin Cook, and Joe Mixon? What are Matt’s RSO building blocks? Russ on a veteran deal or Kyler on a rookie deal? Who would he actually trade Christian McCaffrey for?

Check out many of these topics and more on a jam-packed and high energy episode of All About Reality.

Episode 15: Upside, Upside and Away with Fantasy Points’ Graham Barfield and Scott Barrett

Goody and Luke are back with their most power-packed episode to date. Graham Barfield and Scott Barrett, co-owners and heavy analysis hitters from the newly created website Fantasy Points (Graham formerly of Fantasy Guru, NFL, Rotoworld, and Scott formerly of PFF) come on the podcast to talk about some of their latest work. The four talk about how upside wins championships, Graham’s Yards Created, Sophomore breakouts at the WR position and Strength of Schedule analysis between 2020 and 2019 after reminiscing a bit about their numberFire days with Goody.

Episode 14: The Superflex Edition of The Grocery List (Evergreen): 

Luke and Goody had so much fun doing the single QB version of the start-up auction they decided to pivot to Superflex as well in their next episode based on listener feedback. How does replacing a flex position with a Superflex position change strategies and valuations? How does their inclusion alter some of the other roster moves at the skill positions? Check it out and also get some Dalvin Cook thoughts here too.

They reveal their teams and contracts to each other blindly on the air which leads to some fun and real reactions for their fictional 12 team, 20 player rosters in a PPR league with the following lineup configuration and contracts:
